Tried-To-Be-Brief Intro

My name is Michael (Yanshu Hong), a "sophisticated" junior at Stanford, where there are only two seasons, everyone's favorite summer and the are-u-kidding-me-this-is-damn-California winter. I am a fu-cky person, which means I major in Philosophy (which is fuzzy) and Computer Science (which is tech-y). Yes I am FoB, I am from Shanghai and I always try my best to avoid saying I'm from China because then people ask "where in China??".

I favor changes, the unknown, being fashion-savvy. I love arts, random books (and articles from Wait-but-why), philosophizing, writing, "in-real" Japanese culture (no anime and weeaboo pls), negitoro-don, free chats about intellectual or funny stuff and finally doing unique things with lovely people. I prefer both logical methodology of thinking and sympathetic feeling towards the world (so Myers Briggs is quite paradoxical to me).

I spent my freshman year summer at Tokyo studying Japanese, half of my sophomore year in San Francisco working as full-stack engineer at a fashion e-commerce startup called Style Puzzle. Now I'm back to school, reading Arthur Schopenhauer's pessimism but at the same time feeling super optimistic towards the future.

My own enigma

Every person is an enigma. No exception for me. I am as multifaceted as you can imagine and I've dreamed of pursuing any profession as there can exist.

Once, I wanted to be an engineer, because I printed my first line of Hello World in 4th grade and kept enjoying coding and the empowerment it accompanies along my school years. Once, I wanted to be a philosopher, because I was always the top-one thinker during daily conversations in high school and college. I enjoy developing my own theory of things and put everything into the harmony of logic. Once, I wanted to be a comedian, because I realized I am that person who always makes people laugh, in a smart way or a stupid way. I want my epitaph write "a playboy of language". Once, I wanted to be an artist, because I practiced pencil sketching for 7 years and the well-developed sense of aesthetics manifests itself throughout my life. I think art is as expressive as prose or code is.
